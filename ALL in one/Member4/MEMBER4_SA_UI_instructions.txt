UNDERSTANDING YOUR ALGORITHM
------------------------------
Simulated Annealing (SA) works like this:
  - It starts with a high temperature T = 5.0
  - At high temperature it sometimes accepts bad moves on purpose
    (moving away from the goal) to explore the map
  - Every step the temperature cools down: T = T * 0.98
  - As temperature drops it becomes more focused on the goal
  - If it cannot reach the goal in 600 steps it falls back to A*

This is why SA looks erratic at the start but gets smarter over time.

        import json, pathlib
        data = {"grid": self.grid,
                "colls": {str(k): v
                          for k, v in self.collectibles.items()}}
        pathlib.Path(fname).write_text(json.dumps(data))

    def load_map(self, fname="custom_map.json"):
        import json, pathlib
        p = pathlib.Path(fname)
        if p.exists():
            d = json.loads(p.read_text())
            self.grid = d["grid"]
            self.collectibles = {eval(k): v
                                 for k, v in d["colls"].items()}

  What this does: players can now save their custom map to a file
